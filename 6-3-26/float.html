Sree Apparels is a garment stitching unit that works exclusively for Ramraj, a leading garment company. The primary workflow of Sree Apparels involves receiving trunk pieces, stitching them, and dispatching the completed trunks back to Ramraj. While this process is straightforward in principle, the current method of managing daily operations is entirely manual and depends on handwritten registers. This manual system introduces significant limitations and challenges. Staff management is cumbersome as details such as staff names, roles, attendance, and salary allocation are maintained in notebooks, making retrieval of information slow and prone to inconsistencies. Identifying staff with similar names and verifying their payment history becomes difficult without a proper digital system. Attendance tracking also suffers from inaccuracies, as marking attendance across multiple shifts (morning, afternoon, evening) requires constant manual effort. There is no systematic way to prevent duplication or modification of records, and the lack of a structured process for shift-based attendance affects transparency and reliability in salary calculation. Stitching records are poorly managed since the number of pieces stitched by each tailor is not recorded in real time. As a result, management cannot accurately assess individual performance or verify production levels when calculating salaries. This creates scope for disputes and errors in determining pay based on output. Piece tracking is also handled manually through registers. The arrival of raw trunk pieces, their stitching progress, and the dispatch of finished trunks are recorded by hand. Tracking multiple bundles over time becomes complex, and there is no way to color-code or visually distinguish between 2

dispatched, pending, or partially paid bundles. This lack of clarity often leads to delays and confusion in operation. Salary calculation is one of the most critical challenges in the existing system. Tailors are paid based on the number of pieces stitched, while helpers receive salaries based on their shifts worked. Calculating these salaries manually not only consumes a great deal of time but also increases the risk of errors. Inaccurate salary computation can lead to dissatisfaction among staff and affect overall productivity. The absence of a centralized digital system means that historical data is difficult to retrieve, analysis of staff performance is nearly impossible, and financial records are scattered across registers. As Sree Apparels grows in staff size and workload, these problems will multiply, making it harder to maintain efficiency and fairness in operations. Therefore, there is a pressing need for a software-based solution that can manage staff details, track attendance across shifts, monitor stitching and piece movement, and automate salary calculations. Such a system will eliminate manual inefficiencies, reduce errors, and provide a transparent and scalable approach to handling the daily operations of Sree Apparels. 1.2 OBJECTIVE OF THE PROJECT The main objective of this project is to develop a comprehensive Employee Management and Salary Processing System for Sree Apparels that replaces the existing manual register-based system with an efficient, accurate, and user-friendly digital solution. The project aims to ensure smooth handling of staff information, attendance tracking, stitching records, piece and trunk movement, salary processing, and analytical reporting. The specific objectives of the project are:  To automate staff and work management by maintaining all staff records, attendance data (including shift-based attendance), stitching counts, and salary details in a centralized database. 3  To implement a secure role-based login system that provides different access levels for Admin and Staff, ensuring data privacy and controlled operations.  To improve accuracy and transparency in salary calculation by directly linking attendance, stitching, and trunk records to the payment process.  To introduce analytical and reporting features that allow management to view monthly and yearly summaries, staff performance, and salary trends through visual dashboards and downloadable reports.  To save time and effort by eliminating dependence on handwritten registers and manual computations.  To enable better decision-making by ensuring that historical and real-time data can be easily retrieved, updated, and analyzed whenever required.  To enhance staff satisfaction and trust by ensuring error-free salary payments, proper record maintenance, and transparent operations.  To provide scalability for future enhancements such as income and expense tracking, mobile app integration, and automated payment gateway connectivity.  By fulfilling these objectives, the system streamlines the daily operations of Sree Apparels, enhances productivity, and supports data-driven staff management while laying a strong foundation for ongoing digital transformation. 4 CHAPTER 2 SYSTEM ANALYSIS 2.1 EXISTING SYSTEM At present, the operations at Sree Apparels are managed entirely through manual registers and handwritten entries. Staff details, attendance records, stitching counts, salary allocations, and the inflow and outflow of garment pieces are all maintained in notebooks. The company has around 25 staff members, including tailors and helpers, and their daily activities are recorded by supervisors using pen and paper. Attendance is noted shift-wise, but there is no structured mechanism to prevent duplication or ensure accuracy across Morning, Afternoon, and Evening shifts. Similarly, details about the arrival of trunk pieces from Ramraj, their stitching status, and dispatch are written manually, making it difficult to track bundle progress over time. Salary calculation is also performed manually at the end of each month, where tailors are paid based on the number of pieces stitched and helpers are paid based on shifts worked. Since all operations rely on registers, retrieving past data or verifying staff performance requires going through several books, which is time-consuming and inefficient. The absence of a centralized record system also makes it difficult to ensure data consistency and accountability. As the scale of work and the number of employees increases, the manual system becomes harder to manage effectively. 2.1.1 DISADVANTAGES OF EXISTING SYSTEM  Recording attendance, stitching details, and salaries manually takes considerable time. 5  Retrieving past records involves searching through multiple registers.  Human errors in attendance or stitching records directly affect salary accuracy.  Staff members cannot easily verify how their salary was calculated.  Management has no clear view of staff performance or bundle status in real time.  No systematic way to know which trunk pieces have been received, stitched, dispatched, or paid for.  High risk of misplacing information when multiple bundles are in progress.  Past records cannot be analyzed easily for staff performance, monthly trends, or financial tracking.  Reports cannot be generated without manual effort.  As staff size and workload increase, managing everything with registers becomes nearly impossible.  Expansion of operations would require more manpower just to maintain records. 2.2 PROPOSED SYSTEM The proposed system is an Employee Management and Salary Processing System for Sree Apparels, developed using the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express.js, React.js, Node.js). The system is designed to automate the existing manual processes and provide a centralized, secure, and data-driven digital platform for managing staff, attendance, stitching, piece tracking, salary processing, and performance analysis. 6 In the proposed system, staff details are stored in a centralized database along with their roles, contact details, and identification images. A role-based login system is implemented, allowing secure access for different user types — Admin and Staff. Admins can manage staff records, monitor performance, generate reports, and process salaries, while Staff can view their own attendance and salary details. Attendance management is digitalized and supports multiple shifts (Morning, Afternoon, and Evening), ensuring that duplicate entries are prevented. Tailors’ stitching records are automatically linked with attendance to calculate pay based on the number of pieces stitched. Similarly, helpers’ salaries are computed according to the number of shifts they attend. Piece and trunk tracking are also digitized. Each bundle is recorded with attributes such as bundle number, item type, quantity, payment status, and dispatch status. Color-coded indicators provide a quick visual reference to identify dispatched, pending, or partially paid bundles. Salary processing is simplified through an integrated calculation system where administrators can define per-piece and per-shift rates. The system automatically computes the total salary based on attendance and stitching data and stores finalized salary records for each month. Additionally, an Analytics and Report Dashboard provides visual insights through charts and tables, displaying monthly and yearly summaries of staff attendance, salary distribution, and stitching performance.
